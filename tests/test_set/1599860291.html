'It would be morally correct to leave him': Dean Henderson should be able to finish the season at Sheffield United, claims Chris Wilder... but admits Manchester United could take 'decision' to recall him

  • His loan deal at Bramall Lane expires before the likely end of the campaign

Chris Wilder believes it would be 'morally correct' for Dean Henderson to see out the season at Sheffield United but admitted Manchester United could choose to recall him.

The coronavirus crisis means the Premier League season will probably finish past the ending date initially agreed in the goalkeeper's season-long loan move to Bramall Lane in the summer.

That gives United the opportunity to recall him before the season is over. United are competing with the Blades for a European spot, with Wilder's side just two points behind them in fifth.

Chris Wilder believes it would be 'morally' right for him to see out the campaign there

Speaking on the Keys and Grey Show on BT Sport, Wilder was asked about a pipping United to the top four. He replied: 'Have you just something there about a Champions League place?

'I think we've just got enough points to stay in the Premier League! Competing with Manchester United Rich are you sure?'

When asked about Henderson staying, he added: 'Personally I think it would be morally correct to leave him playing at Sheffield United for the last ten games of the season.

But Wilder did admit that Manchester United could choose to recall him from his loan

'But we all know that because of the legalities and the situation regarding expiry of loans that they [Manchester United] might take that decision.

'But like I said, we're not a threat to Man Utd so I'm sure that they'll be quite happy with the players that they've got to see them through the season. The Ford Fiesta ain't going to get past the Ferrari!'

The 23-year-old has impressed for the South Yorkshire side this campaign, playing 27 Premier League games.

There have been suggestions that United are already viewing him as a future No 1, while big teams across Europe are thought to be keeping a close eye on him.
